Dr. René Favaloro

Favaloro, a prestigious thoracic surgeon, was the creator of the aorto-coronary bypass technique, or myocardial revascularization surgery, which gave him great international prestige, since the procedure radically changed the history of coronary disease.

Here´s a time line of Dr. Favaloro´s life:

  • 1923- Born in La Plata, Buenos Aires.
  • 1949- Graduated from the Faculty of Medical Sciences of the University of La Plata.
  • 1950- Started as an assistant doctor in the Polyclinic Hospital.
  • 1962- He settled in cleveland and concentrated his work on valvular and congenital diseases.
  • 1967- Studied the possibility of using the saphenous vein in coronary surgery, practicing with his ideas in May of that year.
  • 1971- Returned to Argentina, to operate at the Güemes Sanatorium in the Federal Capital.
  • 1975- Founded the Favaloro Fundation.
  • 1983- Receives the Canada Gairdner International Award.
  • 1992- The Institute of Cardiology and Cardiovascular Surgery of the Favaloro Foundation, a non-profit entity, was inaugurated in Buenos Aires.
  • 2000- He takes his life with a shot to the heart.

"I would be satisfied if the hearts of Argentines had three things: honesty, responsability and solidarity."
-René Favaloro.-
